Caldereta Recipe

Caldereta is a classic Filipino stew that’s perfect for any occasion—be it a simple family dinner or a festive weekend gathering. Known for its rich, savory sauce and tender meat, it’s a dish that always brings comfort to the table. What makes this version extra special is the addition of shredded cheese, which melts into the sauce, giving it a creamy richness that both kids and adults will enjoy.

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs. beef neck bones
  • 2 lbs. beef chuck cubed
  • 3 potatoes
  • 3 carrots
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 2 green bell peppers
  • 5 ounces green olives pitted
  • 2 ounces cheddar cheese
  • 2 onions
  • 5 cloves garlic
  • 5 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 8 ounces tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons beef powder
  • 3 tablespoons peanut butter
  • 8 grams Maggi Magic Sarap
  • Ground black pepper to taste
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 cups cooking oil

How To Cook

  1. Marinate beef neck bones and beef chuck in soy sauce for 10 minutes.

  2. Meanwhile, deep-fry potatoes and carrots until lightly browned, then set aside.

  3. In the same oil, sauté onion and garlic until fragrant. Add the beef and cook until lightly browned.

  4. Pour in tomato sauce and water, cover, and boil. Lower the heat and simmer for 40 minutes.

  5. Stir in tomato paste and continue cooking for 20–35 minutes, or until the beef is tender, adding water as needed.

  6. Mix in beef powder, liver spread, and peanut butter, then add olives and bell peppers. Season with Maggi Magic Sarap and black pepper.

  7. Add the fried potatoes and carrots, toss well, and finish with cheese.

  8. Cook for 2–3 minutes, then transfer to a serving bowl. Best served with rice.

Tips & Tricks

  • Tomato sauce: Use a good-quality, plain variety that’s not too sweet or tangy. Avoid Italian-style (too tangy) and Filipino-style (too sweet). Regular or unsalted works best so you can adjust the seasoning yourself.

  • Peanut butter: Choose unsweetened, plain peanut butter for the right balance of flavor. Skip chunky or extra-creamy types, as they can alter the subtle nutty taste and smooth texture of caldereta.
