Paksiw na Bangus Recipe

Paksiw na Bangus is a classic Filipino dish that showcases the love for bold, tangy flavors. It’s made by simmering milkfish in vinegar with garlic, ginger, onions, and spices. Simple and cooked in just one pot, this dish delivers a satisfying meal with vinegar’s sharpness balanced by the warmth of aromatics.

Ingredients

  • 3 1/2 lbs. milkfish cleaned and sliced crosswise into serving pieces
  • 3 ounces pork fat chopped
  • 5 thumbs ginger thinly sliced
  • 1 head garlic crushed
  • 1 ¼ cup white vinegar
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 onions sliced thinly
  • 2 Chinese eggplants sliced
  • 2 bitter melons sliced
  • 5 long green pepper
  • 2 teaspoons whole peppercorn
  • fish sauce to taste

How To Cook

  1. In a pan, sear pork fat until it releases enough oil, then turn off the heat.

  2. Layer garlic, ginger, onion, and peppercorns, followed by milkfish slices, long green peppers, eggplant, and bitter melon.

  3. Pour in vinegar, cover, and boil for 2 minutes. Add water, bring back to a boil, then simmer for 15 minutes.

  4. Season with fish sauce to taste. Transfer to a serving plate and enjoy with rice.

Tips & Tricks

  • Pre-soak the bitter melon. Soak the slices in salted water for 10–15 minutes to help reduce their natural bitterness.

  • Avoid overcooking the fish. Milkfish is delicate and cooks fast; stick to the recommended cooking time to keep the meat tender, moist, and flavorful.
