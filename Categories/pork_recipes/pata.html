Crispy Pata Recipe

Crispy Pata, or deep-fried pork leg, is a well-loved Filipino dish. It’s often enjoyed as a main meal with rice and atchara or served as pulutan with drinks. Best of all, it pairs perfectly with a spicy vinegar dip.

Ingredients

  • 1 piece whole pig leg cleaned
  • 6 pieces dried bay leaves
  • 2 tablespoons whole peppercorn
  • 4 pieces star anise optional
  • 6 teaspoons salt
  • 2 teaspoons ground black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 12 cups water
  • 6 cups cooking oil

How To Cook

  1. Bring water to a boil in a pot and add bay leaves, peppercorns, star anise, and salt.

  2. Add the pork leg and simmer until tender, about 45–60 minutes.

  3. Remove from the pot, let cool, then rub with garlic powder, black pepper, and the remaining salt. Rest for 15 minutes.

  4. In a large pot with hot oil, deep-fry the pork leg over medium heat until crispy on one side, then carefully flip to crisp the other.

  5. Remove from oil and transfer to a serving plate.

  6. Serve with atchara and a soy-vinegar dipping sauce. Enjoy!

Tips & Tricks

  • Boil the pork leg until very tender to achieve the crispiest texture when fried.

  • Be cautious when frying, as the oil will splatter. Stay alert.
