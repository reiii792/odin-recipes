Leche Flan Recipe

Leche flan on its own is already a rich, sweet treat that Filipinos love. But when paired with another classic favorite like puto, it becomes even more irresistible. Leche Puto combines the soft, cake-like texture of steamed rice cakes with the silky creaminess of custard, creating a dessert that’s both comforting and festive. It’s simple to prepare, takes just about an hour, and makes the perfect sweet ending to family dinners or special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 10 pieces eggs
  • 1 can condensed milk 14 oz
  • 1 cup fresh milk or evaporated milk
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Separate the yolks from all the eggs, setting aside the whites (only the yolks will be used).

  2. Place the yolks in a large bowl and beat them lightly with a fork or whisk.

  3. Add the condensed milk and mix until smooth.

  4. Pour in the fresh milk and vanilla, then stir well to combine.

  5. In a llanera (traditional flan mold), melt the sugar over low heat until it turns into golden caramel. Swirl the mold to coat the bottom evenly. Tip: If a llanera isn’t available, you can use small round pans or ramekins instead.

  6. Set aside the mold for about 5 minutes to let the caramel set.

  7. Once ready, pour the egg and milk mixture into the mold. Cover tightly with aluminum foil.

  8. Steam for 30–35 minutes, or until the flan is firm and set.

  9. Serve for dessert. Share and Enjoy!
