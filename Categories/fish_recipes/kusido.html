Kusido Recipe

There’s nothing more comforting than a warm bowl of fish soup on a quiet afternoon. Kusido, made with fresh tilapia and a few simple ingredients, delivers light, clean flavors with a touch of tang. Quick to prepare and deeply satisfying, it’s the perfect dish for busy days, rainy weather, or whenever you crave something warm and familiar.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs. tilapia sliced into serving pieces
  • 3 pieces tomatoes wedged
  • 1 piece onion sliced
  • 3 pieces long green peppers
  • 3 thumbs ginger crushed
  • 1 bunch sweet potato leaves
  • ¼ cup calamansi juice
  • 1 quart water
  • Salt to taste

How To Cook

  1. Bring water to a boil in a pot.

  2. Add tomatoes, onion, ginger, and long green peppers, then simmer for 3 minutes.

  3. Add tilapia and cook for 7–10 minutes. Stir in calamansi juice and season with salt.

  4. Add sweet potato leaves and cook for 1 minute more. Transfer to a serving bowl and enjoy!

Tips & Tricks

  • Use the freshest fish for a cleaner, tastier broth.

  • Don’t overcook the tilapia—once it’s opaque and flakes easily, it’s ready.

  • Crush ginger instead of slicing to release more flavor without leaving large chunks.

  • Add the greens last; sweet potato leaves cook in just a minute and stay vibrant.

  • Taste as you go, adjusting salt and calamansi gradually to suit your preference.
