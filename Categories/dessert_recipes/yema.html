Yema Recipe

Golden, sticky, and irresistibly sweet yema is one of the most recognizable Filipino treats. Its name comes from the Spanish word for “egg yolk,” and its origin has a unique history. During the Spanish colonial period, egg whites were often used in construction to strengthen church walls. This left an abundance of unused yolks, which people later mixed with sugar, giving birth to the candy we now know as yema.

Over time, it became a beloved Filipino staple, with locals adding their own twists. Milk was eventually introduced, simmered down to create that rich, buttery flavor that makes yema even more indulgent.

Ingredients

  • 28 ounces condensed milk equivalent to 2 cans
  • 3 tablespoons peanuts roasted

Instructions

  1. Chop the peanuts and set aside.

  2. In a cooking pot, pour in the condensed milk and bring to a gentle boil.

  3. Stir constantly for about 10 minutes.

  4. Reduce the heat, then add the chopped peanuts. Continue stirring and cooking for another 10 minutes, or until the mixture thickens into a paste-like consistency.

  5. Transfer the yema mixture into a bowl and let it cool slightly.

  6. Wrap the mixture into small portions using pre-cut cellophane (around 4x4 inches works well, but you can cut smaller pieces for bite-sized candies).

  7. Allow to cool completely before serving. Share and enjoy!
